000 – 01

A Quiet Form of Violence

It always felt like being on the outside rather than on the inside.Many challenges, but in truth very few helpful guidelines to counter externally imposed tasks with adequate approaches to solutions,

in order to make the best possible outcome of the situation.

It felt like a mind, a way of thinking,that resembled an aquarium in the shape of a highly restrictive glass sphere. Nothing moved.It was a felt inner idling,within a constantly changing world,from which there seemed to be no escape.

Something fit less and less with what seemed to be required to overcome this nightmarish feeling of being unable to make progress while trying to flee.

An eternal swimming in circles,without finding an exit from one’s own constricting structure of thoughts and lived repetitions that revolved endlessly around themselves.

And although all of this took place within my own head,although I — I and my body — belonged to myself,and thus did not seem entirely alienated from the world as a whole,it did not feel like my life.

And as would later become apparent,I was in fact not living my own life,but rather inside a manipulated, constructed matrix of a life, created by a person who pursued only one purpose to fabricate the illusion of a family and a life

that they themselves were never capable of living.

Realistically, from my position, one assumes the real existence of a normal father figure.But this was different.

This entity, with all of its personal inadequacies,created a construct that might be called a family in a social context, but in the end was nothing more than a puppet theater.

A puller of strings, attempting to reshape family members into marionettes for their own purposes.Family members as personal forced laborers. Individuals as instruments to do what this thing was never capable of doing itself.

One could also say: family members as camouflage before the world, shielding a deeply disturbing personal worldview and profound social incompetence.
